Divvy Statistical Analysis

For those who don't know, Divvy is a bicycle-sharing service that allows users to pick-up and drop-off bicycles all throughout the city. Divvy has brought in an era of a more eco-friendly means of transportation, and it has inspired other companies to do the same. Now that people can bike as part of their commute we can start to decrease our carbon footprint. We want to see the ridership of Divvy throughout the year with different weather conditions, and we want to prove that weather has an effect on ridership using data.

Before we did our hypothesis testing we did some statistical analysis. We used Z scores to filter out any outliers that may be in the data. We also ran T-tests and ANOVA-test to see if there is a significant difference in ridership for good weather days versus bad weather days.

We came up with 5 hypotheses that we would like to test in regards to Divvy ridership. Our hypotheses are:

  1. Bad weather affects the number of rides
  2. Bad weather affects customers more than subscribers
  3. Temperature affects the number of rides
  4. Customers ride less than subscribers in colder temperatures
  5. Stations see different ridership depending on the weather.

The box plot shows us the distribution of the numbers of riders during different temperatures. We see that overall the number of riders decreases as the temperature decrease. This makes sense because when it is cold people are less inclined to hop on a bicycle and more inclined to go in a car/bus etc.

The scatterplot above shows us how temperature has an effect on the number of Divvy riders. As the temperature decreases so do the number of riders. Which completely logical because many people do not like to bike in the cold. We found that Divvy Ridership is highly seasonal so it would interesting to make a time series. We can see the seasonality because the highs in ridership occur in the summer months, while the lows in ridership occur in winter months.

With enough data we can do a possible time series forecast/analysis and try to predict the number of bikers on the platform at any given point in time.

This is a heatmap of city of Chicago. The colored spots represent where the Divvy bikes are picked up or dropped off. We can see on the East side towards the lake there is a lot more biking activity.

One suggestion would be to keep up with maintenance in those high activity areas. Another actionable insight would be to add more bikes in those high activity locations because there tend to be more bikers in that area.
