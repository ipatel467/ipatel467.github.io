Predicting Airfare Prices

Traveling by airplane has become a ubiquitous means of transportation, so the day-old question arises. Can we predict the cost of an airplane ticket? I tried my best to do just that and came up with some satisfying metrics. Just to give an outline of the project, I mainly used Python's Sci-Kit Learn library for the heavy lifting. Sci-Kit Learn provides many different types of machine learning algorithms. Specifically, I used different Supervised Regressor Algorithms because I am trying to predict the cost of an airplane ticket which is a continuous value. I wanted to see how much impact a person's choices can affect the cost of a ticket. That is why I wanted to use some features that can 'manipulated' by the customers.

I used a dataset with over 600k observed flight records. These observations contained data that provided information such as cabin class, departure/arrival location, departure date, time of departure, and day of the week, etc. The majority of these features can be manipulated in some way by the customer. The customer is usually in control of what cabin class they sit in, departure time, flight day of the week, purchased days in advance, etc, because at the end of the day the customer is paying for the ticket. I wanted to see how these features can have an impact on the price of a ticket. Here is a bar chart of the average ticket cost by cabin class.

However, let us not be naive and think these prices are fixed. Flight prices are always subject to change. Two people on the same flight can pay completely different prices for their tickets. To better understand the price variations by cabin-class here are the price distributions for each cabin class.

I made a model that could predict the price of plane tickets. The metrics of evaluation I received were an RMSE of 137.23, and an R^2 of .724. Even though my RMSE is a little high I am still satisfied with my project since it was my first attempt, and I got a decent R^2. My model has an R^2 of .724 which means my model is capturing over 72% of the data needed to predict the price of an airplane ticket. Here are some of the features I used in my model.

The most important feature in my was cabin class. To be more specific, it was economy class. Which makes sense because the price range for an economy class ticket is significantly smaller than business or first class. I reran my model to see which features affect ticket prices the most when cabin class is taken out of the model. The type of airline has the most effect on the price of a ticket. After that, the 'time of day' feature(early morning, noon, late evening, etc) and the 'Saturday' feature have a big effect on price. Saturday is the cheapest day to fly for all cabin classes.
